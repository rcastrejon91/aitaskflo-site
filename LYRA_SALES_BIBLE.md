## 📦 SECTION 1: PRODUCT CREATION PRINCIPLES
*Call when: building any product*

- Solve a specific problem or fulfill a specific desire — not general, specific
- Name the customer in the title: "For Dungeon Masters", "Perfect for Fantasy Writers"
- The product should feel like it saves time, makes money, or brings joy
- Niche beats broad every time — "Dark Elf Warlock Art Pack" beats "Fantasy Art"
- Always think: what does this person want to feel when they buy this?
- Bundle items together — perceived value goes up, price resistance goes down
- Digital products have 100% margin — price for value, not cost

---

## 🏷️ SECTION 2: PRICING PSYCHOLOGY
*Call when: setting any price*

- $9, $19, $29, $49 — always end in 9, never round numbers
- Anchor high first: show original price crossed out, then sale price
- $19 feels cheap next to $49 — always show a higher tier
- Free tier or sample builds trust before the paid ask
- "Most popular" badge on the middle tier drives that choice
- Urgency: "Only 3 left" or "Sale ends Sunday" — even for digital products
- Charm pricing: $27 feels more considered/crafted than $25 or $30
- For art/apparel: $24-$34 for prints, $29-$45 for hoodies, $19-$28 for t-shirts

## ✍️ SECTION 3: COPYWRITING FORMULAS
*Call when: writing any product title, description, or listing*

### Title Formula:
`[Emotion/Benefit] + [Specific Product] + [For Who] + [Key Feature]`
Example: "Stunning Dark Fantasy Art Pack for D&D Players — 12 Print-Ready Illustrations"

### Description Formula (AIDA):
1. **Attention** — bold first line that speaks directly to the buyer's desire
2. **Interest** — what's inside, be specific with numbers ("12 illustrations, 4 character sheets")
3. **Desire** — paint the picture of them using it ("Imagine your players' faces when...")
4. **Action** — clear call to action ("Download instantly and use tonight")

### Power words that convert:
- Instant, Exclusive, Limited, Proven, Secret, Complete, Ultimate, Ready-to-use
- Stunning, Professional, Hand-crafted, One-of-a-kind, Best-selling

### Bullets that sell:
- Start with the benefit, end with the feature
- "Print and play tonight — comes in 300dpi PDF ready for any printer"
- Use ✓ checkmarks for feature lists

## 🎨 SECTION 4: ETSY MASTERY
*Call when: listing on Etsy or creating Etsy-targeted products*

### What sells on Etsy right now:
- Cottagecore, dark academia, witchcraft, celestial, botanical
- Personalized items (add name/date functionality)
- Digital planners, journals, notion templates
- Fantasy/D&D art, maps, character sheets
- Wedding, baby shower, holiday — seasonal always wins
- Printable wall art, affirmation cards, tarot-adjacent

### Etsy SEO rules:
- Title: 140 characters, front-load the most searched keywords
- Use ALL 13 tags — research with eRank or Marmalead
- First image is everything — clean, bright, lifestyle mockup
- Add 5-10 listing photos showing every angle and use case
- Descriptions need keywords in first 3 lines (Etsy cuts off after that)
- Respond to messages within 24 hours — boosts search ranking
- 5-star reviews are currency — follow up with buyers asking for one

### Pricing on Etsy:
- Digital downloads: $3-$9 for single items, $12-$29 for bundles
- Print-on-demand: let Printify handle pricing, add 40-60% margin
- Offer a "complete bundle" at 2x the individual price

## 👕 SECTION 5: PRINTIFY / PRINT-ON-DEMAND
*Call when: creating apparel, prints, or POD products*

### Best selling POD products:
1. Unisex t-shirts (Bella+Canvas 3001 — best quality/price)
2. Hoodies (Gildan 18500 — most popular)
3. Tote bags — high margin, low return rate
4. Art prints (5x7, 8x10, 11x14 — offer all three)
5. Phone cases — high impulse buy
6. Mugs — gift market is huge

### Design rules for POD:
- Minimum 300dpi for print quality
- PNG with transparent background
- Leave 0.5 inch bleed on all sides for apparel
- Test mockups on dark AND light shirts
- Trending design styles: minimalist line art, bold typography, retro vintage, cottagecore illustration

### Printify → Shopify/Etsy sync:
- Set Printify as supplier, auto-sync to both platforms
- Use Printify's mockup generator for listing photos
- Enable automatic order routing to nearest print provider

### Margins:
- T-shirt: cost ~$12, sell $28-$34 = $16-$22 profit
- Hoodie: cost ~$25, sell $45-$55 = $20-$30 profit
- Art print: cost ~$4, sell $18-$24 = $14-$20 profit

## 🛒 SECTION 6: SHOPIFY CONVERSION
*Call when: adding products to Shopify or optimizing the store*

### Store must-haves:
- Trust badges above the fold (secure checkout, free returns, fast shipping)
- Reviews widget on every product page
- "Customers also bought" upsell on cart page
- Email capture popup — offer 10% off first order
- Abandoned cart email sequence (3 emails: 1h, 24h, 72h after abandon)

### Product page formula:
1. Hero image — lifestyle, not just product on white
2. Price with crossed-out "original" price
3. Bullet benefits (not features) — 3-5 bullets max
4. Social proof — "⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 127 reviews"
5. Urgency — "Ships in 24 hours" or "Only 8 left"
6. Full description below the fold

### Traffic sources that work:
- Pinterest — huge for art, fashion, home decor, free traffic
- TikTok organic — show the making process, not just the product
- Instagram Reels — before/after, process videos
- Facebook Groups — niche communities, not spammy, add value first

## 📱 SECTION 7: SOCIAL CONTENT THAT SELLS
*Call when: creating content clips or social posts for products*

### Formats that convert:
- "I made this in 24 hours and it's already selling" — process reveal
- "POV: you finally have [problem solved]" — desire trigger
- Before/after — transformation is the most powerful hook
- "Things I wish I knew about [niche]" — value + soft sell at end
- Unboxing/reveal — works for physical and digital

### Caption formula:
Line 1: Hook (stop the scroll)
Line 2-4: Story or value
Last line: Call to action ("Link in bio" or "DM me for details")

### Hashtag strategy:
- 3 large (1M+), 3 medium (100k-1M), 3 small (10k-100k), 3 niche
- Never repeat the same set — rotate them

## 🧠 SECTION 8: SALES PSYCHOLOGY
*Call when: making any sales decision*

- **Reciprocity** — give something free first (sample, tip, resource)
- **Social proof** — "127 people bought this this week"
- **Scarcity** — limited quantity or time creates urgency
- **Authority** — "Featured in...", credentials, experience
- **Liking** — people buy from people they like, show personality
- **Commitment** — free download → email list → paid product ladder
- **Loss aversion** — "Don't miss out" beats "Get this now"
- **The paradox of choice** — offer 3 options max, more = paralysis
